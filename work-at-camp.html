Work at Camp Orkiss!

Employment Opportunities

Here at Camp Orkiss, all positions are focused on mentoring youth and building community through the 3 R's: Respect for Self, Respect for Others, and Respect for the Environment.

Requirements for all employment at camp:

  • Current CPR and First-Aid certification.
  • Must pass drug test at beginning of employment.
  • Must pass pre-employment background check.

Job Positions:

General Counselor

Minimum age: 18

Counselors live in a cabin with about 8-12 campers and another co-counselor. Counselors are responsible for basic care of campers that are in their cabin for 1 week sessions. There are 8 weekly sessions over the summer. Ages of kids vary from 7-15 years old. Counselors are assigned to an age group at beginning of summer, but may move around to work with other age groups throughout the summer.

Counselors are responsible for creating a safe, positive and supportive environment for all campers. Help encourage campers to participate in activities and appreciate the outdoors. Lead campers in games and learning activities. Counselors work in a group of 6-8 counselors, caring for 30-40 campers, with one supervisor

Horse Counselor

Minimum age: 18

Same job duties as listed above for General Counselor. However, Horse Counselors do not generally get moved around, they work within the Horse Camp Unit all summer with 5 counselors and about 25 campers. Horse Camp is a speciality camp that campers sign up for to learn to ride and care for horses.

Horse Counselors live up near the barn as they and the campers are responsible for helping care for the horses on a daily basis. At least half of each day is spent riding and training with the horses.

Extreme Team Member

Minimum age: 18

Extreme Team Members do not live with campers. They live in a cabin with other support staff. Extreme Team Members are responsible for facilitating all low and high rope challenge courses, the zip-line and giant swing. They undergo intensive training to learn how to facilitate these activies safely. Will learn how to belay.

Must feel comfortable with heights and being physically active.

Preparing for Camp

First of all, prepare to have the summer of your life!

Since Camp Orkiss is located on Orcas Island, you must drive to Anacortes and then drive on or walk onto the ferry that will bring you to Orcas Island. Do not worry if you walk on, a camp bus will be on the other side to pick you.

What you should plan to bring to spend a summer at camp and in the outdoors:

  • Clothes appropriate to work outdoors in both rain and shine. Including warm clothing as it gets chilly in the evenings. Sandals or flip-flops are okay most of time, but must have closed-toe shoes to ride horses and participate on challenge courses.
  • Your own toiletries, including sunscreen! Bathrooms are very basic, no blow-dryers or anything provided.
  • Bedding: sleeping bag and pillow and sheet if you want one on your mattress pad.
  • Backpack to lug stuff around.
  • Water bottle, must stay hydrated!
  • Flashlight, it gets dark outside when you need to walk from your cabin to the bathrooms.
  • Battery-operated alarm clock.
